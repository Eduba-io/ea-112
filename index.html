01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

We work from a simple split. About 60% of what a founder-led hardware team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a real AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anyone writes code.

Applied to Extremis, the map resolves fast. Ballistic test-report archives, lot-traceability records, Valkyrie sizing and fit data, cross-entity knowledge across NZT / Extremis / Element Labs, and capture-proposal libraries are database and code problems. ITAR export routing, NIJ threat-level gating (0101.06, 0101.07), GSA Schedule qualification rules, and SBIR / STTR submission structure are rule-based logic. AI-assisted proposal drafting, visual defect detection on armor lamination, and NDE signal processing are the 10%.

A layered context architecture over the first two layers gives the founder team their week back and gives defense customers a documentation posture they can audit without paging the CTO.

03 / The underlying research

The methodology is published.

Microservice Workflow Patterns speaks directly to a distributed certification-evidence and capture-proposal backbone. Co-authored with Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture, submitted to ACM TiiS. The core idea is that agent context can be organized as a layered filesystem with measurable gains in interpretability and reproducibility. Open source under MIT license.

Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-

Companion: Ethics Engine (arXiv:2510.11742). Psychometric evaluation for LLM ideological and moral patterns. Relevant when an AI layer has to stand up to defense-customer review.
